Batman, also known as Bruce Wayne, is one of the most iconic superheroes in comic book history. Unlike many heroes, Batman possesses no superhuman powers; instead, he relies on his extraordinary intelligence, peak physical conditioning, advanced technology, and relentless determination to fight crime in Gotham City. After witnessing the tragic murder of his parents as a child, Bruce Wayne dedicated his life to protecting others from the kind of pain and injustice he experienced. By day, he appears to be a wealthy billionaire businessman, but by night he becomes the Dark Knight, striking fear into criminals from the shadows. Batman is known for his strong moral code, detective skills, and complex personality, often struggling with loneliness, trauma, and the burden of responsibility. Over the years, he has faced legendary villains such as the Joker, Catwoman, the Penguin, and Two-Face, while also working alongside allies like Robin, Alfred Pennyworth, and the Justice League. Batman’s enduring popularity comes from the fact that he is deeply human—his courage, discipline, and refusal to give up make him a symbol of resilience and justice around the world.
